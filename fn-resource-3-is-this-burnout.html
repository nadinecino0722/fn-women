Is this burnout?
How to tell — and what comes next.

A decision-support guide for founders who aren't sure if what they're feeling is a hard stretch or something that needs more serious attention.

3 min read All stages Burnout · Self-awareness · Action

First, a distinction worth making: Every founder has hard weeks. Pressure, exhaustion, and doubt are part of the job. Burnout is different — it's what happens when those experiences become chronic and start affecting your ability to function. The checklist below is designed to help you tell the difference. Check anything that feels true for you, consistently, over the past month or more.

Emotional & mental signals

These are signs that your capacity for engagement and resilience has been significantly depleted.

  • I feel emotionally flat or numb — not sad exactly, just switched off.
  • Things that used to excite me about the company no longer do.
  • I'm more cynical or detached than I used to be — about the work, the team, or the mission.
  • Small decisions feel disproportionately hard or overwhelming.

Physical & behavioural signals

Burnout shows up in the body and in patterns of behaviour, not just in how you think and feel.

  • I'm sleeping more than usual but still waking up exhausted — or barely sleeping at all.
  • I've been getting sick more often, or taking longer to recover when I do.
  • I'm relying more heavily on alcohol, caffeine, or other crutches to function.
  • I've been avoiding people — team, investors, friends — more than I normally would.

Performance & work signals

These signals show up in the quality and character of the work itself.

  • My judgment feels off — I'm second-guessing decisions I'd normally make with confidence.
  • I'm less patient with my team than I should be, or catching myself after reactions that aren't like me.
  • I'm procrastinating significantly more than usual, even on things that matter.
  • I'm putting in long hours but feel like I'm producing less than I used to.

What comes next — depending on where you are

◎

You checked 0–3: This looks like a hard stretch, not burnout

Pressure and fatigue are real, but they're not the same as burnout. The most useful thing you can do right now is protect your recovery before depletion compounds. One concrete boundary, one night of real sleep, one conversation with someone who gets it — these are not luxuries. They're maintenance.

◎

You checked 4–7: You're in the amber zone — pay attention

This isn't full burnout, but the pattern is pointing in that direction. What you do in the next few weeks matters. Identify one or two specific things you can change — a standing commitment you cancel, a weekend you protect, a conversation you've been avoiding. If things don't improve in a month, treat this more seriously.

◎

You checked 8 or more: This deserves serious attention

At this level, self-managed recovery is unlikely to be enough on its own. This is not a judgment — it's a practical observation. Burnout at this intensity typically requires a real structural change (to your workload, your support, or your schedule) and often benefits from talking to someone outside your immediate circle. The Navigate Care section of the FN library can help you think through options — without FN directing you to any specific provider.

◎

Regardless of your score: tell someone

Not necessarily everything. Not necessarily a professional. But the research on founder mental health is consistent: isolation makes it worse, and being witnessed — even once, by even one person — makes it better. If you don't know who that person is, that's worth noticing too.

Important: This guide is a reflection and decision-support tool — it is not a clinical assessment and does not constitute medical or psychological advice. If you are experiencing thoughts of self-harm or are in crisis, please contact a mental health professional or crisis service directly.
